Sheikh Zayed Grand Mosque

The Sheikh Zayed Grand Mosque is one of the world’s largest mosques1and is located in Abu Dhabi, UAE2. It was built between 1996 and 2007 and takes its name from Sheikh Zayed bin Sultan, the founder of the United Arab Emirates2. The mosque features 82 domes, more than 1,000 columns, 24-carat-gold gilded chandeliers and the world's largest hand-knotted carpet1. It can welcome up to 55,000 worshippers and visitors every day3. The mosque was built to embody Islam’s foremost message of peace and tolerance and welcome people of all beliefs3.
